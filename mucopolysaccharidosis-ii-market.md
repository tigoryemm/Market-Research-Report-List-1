<p><p>Mucopolysaccharidosis II, also known as Hunter syndrome, is a rare genetic disorder resulting from the deficiency of the enzyme iduronate-2-sulfatase. This deficiency leads to the accumulation of glycosaminoglycans in the body, causing progressive cellular and tissue damage. Symptoms often emerge in early childhood and can include developmental delays, joint stiffness, and various physical deformities. Currently, treatment options are limited, with enzyme replacement therapy being the most common approach to manage the condition. </p><p>The Mucopolysaccharidosis II Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 9.8% during the forecast period.
