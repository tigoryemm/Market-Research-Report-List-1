<p><p>The artificial tendons market is primarily categorized into two types: in vitro tissue and internal tissue. In vitro tissue refers to engineered tendons created outside the body, often used for research and testing purposes. These tendons allow for the study of tendon properties and potential treatments in a controlled environment. Conversely, internal tissue pertains to artificial tendons designed for implantation within the human body, aimed at restoring function and repairing damaged tendons in patients. Both types play critical roles in advancing tendon repair and rehabilitation.</p></p>
