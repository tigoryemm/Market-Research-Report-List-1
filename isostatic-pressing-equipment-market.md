<p><p>The isostatic pressing equipment market is divided into two main types: cold isostatic pressing (CIP) and hot isostatic pressing (HIP). Cold isostatic pressing utilizes pressure at room temperature to shape materials, ideal for enhancing density and uniformity in products like ceramics and metals. In contrast, hot isostatic pressing applies high temperature alongside pressure, enabling materials to achieve advanced mechanical properties and refining microstructures. Both methods serve critical roles in manufacturing, particularly in aerospace, automotive, and tooling applications.</p></p>
