<p><p>Photoplethysmography (PPG) biosensors are optical-based devices that detect blood volume changes in microvascular tissues, commonly used for heart rate monitoring and other cardiovascular assessments. These non-invasive sensors utilize light to measure the variations in light absorption caused by pulsatile blood flow. With the increasing prevalence of cardiovascular diseases and a heightened focus on remote patient monitoring, the demand for PPG biosensors is rapidly expanding.</p><p>The market for PPG biosensors is expected to grow at a CAGR of 7.8% during the forecast period. This growth is driven by advancements in technology, integration with mobile health applications, and the rising adoption of wearable devices. Key trends include the development of more sophisticated algorithms for better accuracy, the miniaturization of sensors, and enhanced connectivity features that allow real-time health monitoring. Additionally, the trend towards home healthcare and telemedicine is fueling the adoption of PPG biosensors, as they provide convenient and efficient monitoring solutions. Overall, the PPG biosensors market is positioned for significant growth, driven by technological innovation and increasing consumer health consciousness.</p></p>
